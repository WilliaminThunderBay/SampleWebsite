About Oshkaatisak

All Young People's Council

Oshkaatisak represents the voice of NAN youth and promotes youth empowerment, engagement, leadership, and hope across NAN First Nations.

Council Role

Youth Voice in NAN Governance

Oshkaatisak, also known as the All Young People's Council, is one of Nishnawbe Aski Nation's advisory councils. It brings youth perspectives to Chiefs Assemblies, Executive Council discussions, policy priorities, and community initiatives.

The council focuses on advocacy, youth success, leadership development, healthy behaviours, resource coordination, and meaningful participation for young people from NAN territory.

Who We Are

Part of Nishnawbe Aski Nation

Oshkaatisak is part of Nishnawbe Aski Nation advisory council structure. NAN represents 49 First Nations across James Bay Treaty No. 9 and the Ontario portion of Treaty No. 5.

The council helps make sure youth priorities are present in governance, gatherings, advocacy, and community conversations.

Mandate & Governance

How Oshkaatisak Works

Oshkaatisak is authorized through NAN Chiefs-in-Assembly resolutions and participates as a meaningful youth voice in NAN governance.

📜

Resolution 12/52

Recognizes the importance of Oshkaatisak and calls for meaningful youth engagement in NAN assemblies.

🪶

Resolution 22/21

Affirms Oshkaatisak as a voice of youth from across NAN First Nations territory.

🤝

Guidance to Leadership

Council members provide guidance and advice to NAN Chiefs and NAN Executive Council.

📣

Advocacy Portfolios

Members contribute through areas such as health, education, justice, wellness, housing, environment, and governance.

🏕️

Youth Gatherings

Annual gatherings bring NAN youth together for connection, culture, learning, leadership, and celebration.

🌈

Inclusion

Council terms require Two-Spirit representation and support belonging for Two-Spirit and gender-diverse youth.
